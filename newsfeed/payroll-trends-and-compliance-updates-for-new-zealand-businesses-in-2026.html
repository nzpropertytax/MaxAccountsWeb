Payroll Trends & Compliance Updates for New Zealand Businesses in 2026

By Manju Davis22 Jan 2026

Staying compliant with payroll obligations is essential for every New Zealand employer. In 2026, several key updates are shaping how businesses manage payroll, from legislative changes to technology-driven improvements. These updates help employers stay accurate, efficient, and aligned with NZ employment law.

Minimum Wage Adjustments (Effective 1 April 2026)

  • Adult Minimum Wage: $23.95/hour
  • Starting-Out & Training Wage: $19.16/hour

These increases apply to part-time, full-time, casual, and fixed-term employees. Employers must update payroll systems, employment agreements, and budgeting forecasts to reflect the new rates.

Holidays Act Reform (2026 Rollout)

The long-awaited Holidays Act reform aims to simplify leave calculations and reduce employer errors. Key changes include:

  • A shift to an hours-based leave model
  • Removal of the parental leave “penalty”
  • Stronger Labour Inspectorate enforcement

Businesses should review leave balances, audit historical data, and ensure their payroll software supports the new calculation method.

KiwiSaver Contribution Rate Changes

From 1 April 2026, the default employee contribution rate increases:

  • 3% → 3.5% (2026)
  • Planned increase to 4% in 2028

Employers should prepare for higher payroll costs and ensure payroll settings reflect the new rates.

Employment Relations Amendment Bill (2026)

This bill introduces several important changes:

  • A clearer contractor vs employee classification test
  • Limits on unjustified dismissal claims for high-income earners
  • Stronger pay transparency obligations

Employers should review contracts, job descriptions, and internal processes to ensure compliance.

Privacy Act Update (IPP 3A – Effective 1 May 2026)

Businesses must now notify individuals when collecting personal information from third-party sources. This affects:

  • Recruitment
  • Payroll onboarding
  • Background checks

Employers should update privacy policies and train staff on new disclosure requirements.
